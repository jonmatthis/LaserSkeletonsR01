## Significance
### Integration of visual and motor systems in walking
- Walking in complex natural environments requires the integration of visual and motor systems.
- Visual search and biomechanics of bipedal locomotion have been studied separately.
- Recent work has started to integrate the understanding of visuomotor control of walking.

### Gap 1: Understanding visual processing in real-world human movement
- Need to understand how visual processing supports human movement in real-world scenarios.
- Current studies lack the sequence of events that occur in the real world.
- Large datasets with eye movements and world content are available, but actions need to be included for better learning.

### Gap 2: Lack of technology to study visuomotor control
- Current technology is insufficient to study visuomotor control in walking.
- Researchers are uniquely positioned to address this gap.

### Goals
- Leverage improved processes for collecting integrated visuomotor datasets to gather more precise and expanded datasets.
- Develop laboratory protocols to study visuomotor control in walking.

### Previous work has shown that walkers fixate upcoming footholds, particularly in difficult terrains. This aim aims to measure and model the fixation strategies used by walkers during foothold finding and evaluate how these strategies are influenced by biomechanical constraints.

## General Methods
### Participants will be recruited and screened to include healthy young adults. Data collection will rely on a mobile eye tracker, a body motion suit, and a lightweight computer. The dataset will be relatively large to establish estimates of individual variability and support effective modeling efforts.

## Technical Deliverables
### The grant will contribute to the production of open-hardware, open-source data processing pipelines and a well-documented visuomotor dataset for visually-guided walking.

## Research Design
### The resulting data will be used to answer questions about visual perception, gaze, gait, the environment, and the coordination of these during walking. Specific research questions include the allocation of gaze during foothold finding, the modulation of gaze allocation by step-to-step gait efficiency, the factors that make a "good" foothold, the individual variability in ground clearance, and the impact of divided attention on visuomotor control of walking.

## Experimental Concerns
### Validated in previous studies and measures will be taken to address potential learning effects.

## Rationale:
The need for laboratory-precision measurements is justified based on the following reasons:
- Accuracy is important as the kinematics of IMU systems cannot be trusted.
- Controllable and parameterizable terrain allows for testing specific hypotheses.
- Indoor work is necessary to test hypotheses generated from outdoor work, which is quasi-observational.

## General Methods:
The Augmented Reality Ground-Plane (ARGP) is a 14m-long indoor walking path that uses projectors to display terrain. The content is displayed using a Unity game engine that streams real-time kinematic data via websocket. The ARGP allows for real-time interactive updates of displayed terrain and provides auditory feedback to participants for high accuracy foot placement. Videos with higher spatial/temporal resolution are also recorded for later analysis. Participants will be recruited from the Northeastern Community.

### Gaze/Gait relationship in terrain of various foothold density:
- Experiment #1: Foothold density manipulation with 5 levels of density.
- Experiment #2: Foothold density manipulation with distractors added to match visual density of the "dense" condition.
- Experiment #3: Examination of gaze/foothold patterns from Aim 1 and Experiments 1/2 to determine typical patterns. Contrived paths with dead-zones and funnels will be created to study gaze/gait patterns and subject reactions.

## Analyses:
- Hypothesis to test: Are fixation patterns driven by biomechanics or vision? Baseline modeling will be conducted to analyze gaze/gait integration, basic stats (look ahead distance, deviations from PGC, speed fluctuations, fixation duration), body-centered factors (LIP model, preferred gait cycle), and retinal-centered factors (retinal optic flow div/curl, visual search using RV1 model).

## Specific Aims
### SA1: Information gathering and motor planning during full-body movement through real-world environments
- Lack of normative baseline data on how individuals use vision to guide walking in complex environments
- Collect integrated visuomotor dataset in 50 adults with typical vision and motor function
- Analyze motor planning strategies, gaze behavior, and coordination of gaze and gait during visually-guided walking
- Technical deliverables: comprehensive visuomotor dataset, open-source documentation of hardware infrastructure, open-source software processing pipeline

### SA2: Testing the spatial and temporal dynamics of visual information gathering and motor planning
- Develop augmented reality ground plane for presenting walking paths
- Manipulate availability of visual information based on body position and gaze location
- Identify critical information for foothold selection during walking
- Technical deliverables: open-source hardware specifications for augmented reality ground-plane, open-source software processing pipeline

### SA3: Impact of Divided on Attention on the visuomotor control of walking
- Measure impact of divided attention on coordination of gaze and gait during full-body movement
- Participants will engage in divided attention tasks while walking
- Insight into allocation of visual information gathering and motor planning resources during multitasking
- Technical deliverables: divided attention extension to comprehensive visuomotor dataset
